About Me


I'm a graduate student at Florida State University in the final stages of earning my Master of Science in Economics degree. Originally from the Atlanta area, I earned my Bachelor's of Business Administration in Economics at Georgia Southern University, along with a minor in Mathematical Sciences, before moving to Florida. There, I was a Research Assistant my senior year after working as the Economic Research intern for the Savannah Economic Development Authority in conjunction with the World Trade Center Savannah Internship Program.

My experience in graduate school has primarily been focused on econometric regression analysis and it's applications. I've completed various independent research projects throughout my coursework by utilizing the theory and models taught in the classroom to analyze real world datasets such as the CDC's Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System.

My goal is to take the skills I've acquired throughout my education and use them as a professional analyst, asking important questions and then providing clear and concise answers using data.
